# Meta-Archetype System
## A Framework for Navigating Humanity-Scale Change

This meta-framework holds multiple archetype systems simultaneously, allowing changemakers to understand themselves, their work, their organizations, and their relationships within the larger ecosystem of transformation.

---

## The Eight Archetype Systems

| # | System | Core Question |
|---|--------|---------------|
| 1 | **Changemaker Archetypes** | What are you best at? |
| 2 | **Shadow Archetypes** | What is your failure mode? |
| 3 | **Community Archetypes** | How do you want to live and belong? |
| 4 | **Change Network Archetypes** | Which movement do you work through? |
| 5 | **Transition-Temporal Archetypes** | Where are you in your journey, and at what timescale? |
| 6 | **Organizational Archetypes** | What kind of organizational culture do you thrive in? |
| 7 | **Resource Archetypes** | How does your work get fueled? |
| 8 | **Knowledge Archetypes** | How do you generate and transmit understanding? |

Plus cross-cutting systems:

| # | System | Core Question |
|---|--------|---------------|
| 9 | **Scale Archetypes** | At what geographic and social scale does your work operate? |
| 10 | **Failure Archetypes** | What breakdown patterns should you watch for? |
| 11 | **Relationship Archetypes** | How does your archetype relate to others in the ecosystem? |

## How the Systems Relate

The meta-archetype system works as concentric layers of self-understanding:

### Layer 1: Inner Dimension (Who You Are)
- **Changemaker Archetypes** → Your strengths and gifts
- **Shadow Archetypes** → Your blind spots and failure modes

### Layer 2: Relational Dimension (How You Live and Belong)
- **Community Archetypes** → Intentional communities, ecovillages, living arrangements
- **Organizational Archetypes** → The internal culture you thrive in

### Layer 3: Work Dimension (What You Do and How)
- **Change Network Archetypes** → The movement or mechanism you work through
- **Resource Archetypes** → How your work is fueled
- **Knowledge Archetypes** → How you generate and transmit understanding

### Layer 4: Systemic Dimension (Where and When)
- **Scale Archetypes** → Geographic and social scale of your work
- **Transition-Temporal Archetypes** → Your journey and timescale

### Layer 5: Ecosystem Dimension (How You Fit)
- **Relationship Archetypes** → How your archetype connects to others
- **Failure Archetypes** → What to watch for and prevent

## How Changemappers Uses This Framework

When someone arrives at Changemappers, the meta-archetype system helps them:

1. **Discover** their changemaker archetype and shadow—what they're best at and what to watch for
2. **Find** their community archetype—what kind of living and belonging they're drawn to
3. **Identify** their change network archetype—which of the twelve movements resonates
4. **Understand** their transition-temporal archetype—where they are in their journey
5. **Navigate** organizational culture—what kind of structure they'll thrive in
6. **Connect** with complementary archetypes—who they need to work with
7. **Anticipate** failure modes—what patterns to watch for in their work

The platform then connects them—practically and specifically—with the people, organizations, resources, and knowledge that match their archetype profile.

## Design Principles

1. **No archetype is superior.** Every archetype is essential to the ecosystem.
2. **Complexity is a feature.** The goal is not to reduce people to a type, but to give them a navigable map.
3. **Archetypes evolve.** People move through different archetypes over their changemaking journey.
4. **Shadow is not failure.** Understanding your shadow is the beginning of integration, not a mark of inadequacy.
5. **Relationships between archetypes matter as much as the archetypes themselves.** The ecosystem is the unit of transformation.
6. **The framework serves the work, not the other way around.** These are tools for navigation, not boxes for identity.
